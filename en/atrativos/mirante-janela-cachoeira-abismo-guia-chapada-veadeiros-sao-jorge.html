Janela Viewpoint and Cachoeira do Abismo

Hire a local guide!

Distance from Alto Paraíso: 31 km (With an additional 4 km of dirt road)

Trail Distance (round trip): 8 kilometers

Difficulty Level: difficult

Attractions:

- Waterfall of the abyss

- Window Viewpoint

- Panoramic view of the national park

Prohibited: R$40

Recommended period for visiting:

From October to April (Cachoeira do Abismo is seasonal)

It is recommended to go with a local guide

THEWindow LookoutIt is a place blessed by God with the most beautiful view of Chapada dos Veadeiros and perhaps Brazil. From the highest point of the mountain, a 360º panoramic view without any construction or human intervention, just the native savanna of the national park with the 120 and 80 meter falls of the Rio Pretinho in an immensity of blue sky. This is a special itinerary because there is no specific time to visit the attraction. Special at all times, be it sunset or sunrise, day or night... at every time with its grandeur of the moment. A rustic bleacher made of rain-resistant wood brings comfort and safety to rest high in the sky.

Heavy trail

The route to the Janela viewpoint is extremely difficult and is one of the most intense in Chapada dos Veadeiros. Vertical slopes and slopes, stairs at critical points and small climbs mark the route. It is an inhospitable place where people constantly get lost, so it is essential to hire a local tour guide to lead the tour safely.

Cachoeira do Abismo is seasonal

Halfway there is a seasonal waterfall that only appears during the rainy season, which runs from October to April. The Abismo waterfall is located on a large slope and that is why it got its name. With a shallow well and a drop of approximately 20 meters, it has a great view of the valley.

Recommended period for visiting

The best time to visit the place would be from the beginning of the rainy season until mid-April, as the waterfall in the abyss is only full during this period. It is possible that there will be no water in April, which will depend on the volume of rain.

Tips

- Go with a local tour guide, the place is difficult to access and very easy to get lost - Bring sunscreen and water as it is an inhospitable place - take a flashlight if you want to stay longer to enjoy the stars - Charge your cell phone before going, there is signal up there so in any emergency you will be able to make calls
