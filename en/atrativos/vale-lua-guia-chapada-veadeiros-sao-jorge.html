Buy your tour!Vale da Lua is 35 km from Alto Paraíso towards São Jorge, 4 km of which are dirt roads. Along the route there will be Jardim de Maytréa, a place with several buritis trees that are located in a humid area and serves as a water tank for the entire Chapada dos Veadeiros region. Heritage that needs to be preserved.

Vale da Lua, the lunar soil of Chapada dos Veadeiros

It gets its name from the peculiar rock formation that looks like the surface of the moon with several smooth, circular craters. It is one of the most visited places in Chapada dos Veadeiros as it is a mystical place. It was millions of years that nature sculpted this monument, creating perfect curves. In the attraction there are natural pools that look more like hot tubs. A true work of art. The distance from the trail to the São Miguel River, which is part of Vale da Lua, is approximately 650 meters and an easy trail. The journey is downhill and the return is uphill. Halfway there is a viewpoint overlooking the valley with a beautiful mountain range.

Natural Pools

At the end of the route, signs will indicate the two main natural pools for swimming. The first is better and during the rainy season it will probably be submerged and it will not be possible to bathe. The most popular pool is the last one, which is even more secure as there are lifeguards. During the rainy season it is very dangerous due to the high number of water heads, so it is necessary to be alert at all times. The Vale da Lua pools in Chapada dos Veadeiros have very cold water, and the best time for it to be a little warmer would be in the months of August, September and October, which marks the end of winter and there is still sun throughout the day. Most of the year during the dry season, the pool wells are shallow. There is also a secret cave that looks like a whirlpool, only guides or locals know where it is.

*Security measures*

- In case of rain, stay away from the canyons and get out of the water to avoid flooding. Lifeguards will be there to warn you, but avoid dangerous places. - Avoid traveling along the river canyon because these rocks are very slippery. Several accidents have already occurred, including fatal ones. Danger signs will be throughout the attraction. Do not exceed areas marked by ropes and respect the signposts. - Under no circumstances go wearing flip-flops, any stumble could be fatal.
