Loquinhas Waterfall

Hire a local guide!

Distance from Alto Paraíso: 4 km

Trail Distance (round trip): 2 km

Difficulty Level: Easy

Attractions:

- Cachoeira das Loquinhas

- Cachoeira do Sol (violet trail)

- Shaman's Well

- Several other small wells with crystal clear water

- Trail made on wooden deck

- Gallery forest

Entry cost: R$50

Recommended period for visiting:

All year round, however the Loquinhas and Sol waterfalls are without water during the dry season (April to October)

Recommended tour for all ages

Cachoeira das Loquinhas, located in Chapada dos Veadeiros, is certainly a trip for the whole family. The place is simply fantastic with several crystal clear wells under an untouched gallery forest. There is great infrastructure and the trail is entirely made on wooden decks. This is a tour for all ages.

Close to town with easy, shaded trail

Located very close to the city of Alto Paraíso, 4km away, Cachoeira das Loquinhas has a very easy difficulty level, so both seniors and children can do the route without any problems. Furthermore, its journey is short, totaling approximately 2 km round trip. Furthermore, the entire trail is covered on wooden decks with safety ropes, making the tour even more peaceful and relaxing for everyone.

Three small trails with crystal clear waters

The place has three trails: Loquinhas, Violeta and Ruby. All of them have signage along the route so there is no risk of getting lost. If this happens, just return via the wooden deck without any problems. The three trails total eighteen small wells and waterfalls along the route, which can be done peacefully while walking in the fully shaded gallery forest. Calm, crystal-clear emerald-colored waters enchant everyone. The water is very cold so go between 9 am and 3 pm to enjoy the heat of the day.

Recommended visiting period and individual fee

Visitation is permitted throughout the year, however the waterfalls become more exuberant during the rainy season as the river is loaded.  However, avoid visiting on rainy days as the attraction is closed as the wooden floor becomes slippery. During the extreme drought that lasts from August to October, the main waterfalls dry up. Children up to 12 years old are free. Parking is ample, safe and free.
