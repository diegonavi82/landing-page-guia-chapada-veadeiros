Angels and Archangels Waterfall

Hire a local guide!

Distance from Alto Paraíso: 16 km (All route on dirt road)

Trail Distance (round trip): 3 km

Difficulty Level: medium (there is a difficult descent at the end of the Arcanjos waterfall)

Attractions:

- Cachoeira dos Anjos

- Archangels Waterfall

- Virtudes Waterfall

- Natural Pools

Entries: R$30

Recommended period for visiting:

Whole year (In heavy rain it is not possible to enter the attraction)

Dogs are allowed

It is recommended to go with a local guide

A little off the traditional tourist route of Chapada dos Veadeiros, the Anjos and Arcanjos waterfalls are certainly worth a visit. The two beautiful waterfalls of the Rio Pretinho, carved by the waters in the middle of a mountain, offer great swimming holes. The attraction also allows dogs, one of the few places that allow these animals. Quiet, little visited and beautiful in nature.

First stop: Cachoeira dos Anjos

The Anjos waterfall is the first attraction and is below the Arcanjos waterfall. In a light walk of just 1.5 km, turn right and it is now possible to bathe in its waters and enjoy the beautiful view and rock formations of the place. Formed under a mini canyon, it has a large pool and its drop is approximately 30 meters. The waters are very cold all year round.

Archangels Waterfall

The second waterfall in the order of arrival that is higher up is the Arcanjos waterfall. It is possible to swim in a spacious well of water that is slightly dark, but extremely clean and also cold. The trail offers plenty of shade, as well as being pleasant to walk. There is a large natural bath next to the waterfall that is only possible to enter during the dry season. Arcanjos offers a larger well for swimming with great depth, and the falling waters form small stairs along the river's descent.

How to Get There

Leaving Alto Paraíso, it is 14 km to reach the village of Moinho, where the Anjos and Arcanjos waterfalls are located. From arriving at the Moinho you need to travel another 2 km, however, there are signposts until you reach the location. The road to the Moinho is quite winding, but on the other hand it has a beautiful view of the cerrado. The trail itself is poorly marked, so you need to pay attention, especially to get to Arcanjos. In this section there are small challenges at the end of the trail, as there are many rocks to climb and descend. Very close to the waterfall there is a descent of rocks with ropes to help. You need to descend very carefully. The final stretch is difficult to access but short.
