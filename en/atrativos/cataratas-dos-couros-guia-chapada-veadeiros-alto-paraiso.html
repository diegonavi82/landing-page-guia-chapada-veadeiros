Buy your tour!The Cataratas dos Couros represents everything tourists are looking for in a single trip to Chapada dos Veadeiros. There are several natural pools, typical geological formations, native vegetation of the cerrado, canyons, waterfalls, small beaches, viewpoints... The Falls are formed by the Rio dos Couros and have four large waterfalls in their attraction: Muralha waterfall, Almécegas 1000 waterfall, Parafuso waterfall and Buracão waterfall. A wall of rocks forms the canyons and intensifies the magic of this place.

Paradise of natural pools

Along the entire route of Cataratas dos Couros there are pools of clear, icy water with small waterfalls. All falls have very calm and safe pools for swimming during the dry season. The different pools make us lose track of time and want to stay in this paradise all day.

How to get there

From Alto Paraíso the path to Catarata dos Couros covers 20 km of asphalt with a further 35 km of dirt road. Taking an average of 1h15m by car. Entrance is free, but there are people who take care of parking on site and contributions are suggested to provide greater security. The trail is 3 km to the last attraction and is considered difficult. All of it is made along the banks of the Rio dos Couros.

Muralha Waterfall

Arriving at the first waterfall, which is just 500 meters from the parking lot, Cachoeira da Muralha, which has a huge horizontal stone wall reminiscent of the Great Wall of China. This is a great place for bathing and relaxing, being the gateway to the journey.

Almécegas 1000 waterfall and Parafuso waterfall

To reach the Almécegas 1000 waterfall, you must travel a distance of approximately 1.5km along the banks of the Couros river. Along the way there will be several small falls with natural pools and small beaches. When the river breaks into a large cliff and forms a huge fall with several steps, this will be the Almécegas 1000 waterfall. One of the most beautiful postcards of Chapada dos Veadeiros. The sound of falling water is deafening. At the end of it, all the water that descends in steps will funnel, forming a very powerful and dangerous fall known as a screw. It is on the bottom floor, so to get there, you will need to walk again. It is not recommended under any circumstances to swim until the screw falls as there is a strong current in that location. The Parafuso waterfall has this name because a whirlpool forms underneath the fall. There have been cases of people drowning in this place because they were sucked in by the current, so stay away from this well.

Buracão stone plateau and waterfall

The end of the trail will be on a huge stone plateau inside the canyons of Cataratas dos Couros. This is a sacred place with incredible views of the Parafuso, Almécegas 1000 and Buracão waterfalls and a beautiful natural pool. Access to the Buracão waterfall can only be done by swimming. For those who want to venture out, it is a great opportunity to enjoy the wonderful view of the canyon cliff that forms a rainbow every morning around 10 am.

Couros Falls Viewpoint

There is an optional part of the tour which is the Couros Falls viewpoint. To get there the trail increases by 1.5km with an extreme level of difficulty. The viewpoint is undoubtedly the most beautiful view of the entire complex, with a privileged view.  It's not easy to get there, so it's not recommended for the elderly or children, and people with restrictions.

Period for visiting and hiring a guide

The Couros Falls in Chapada dos Veadeiros is certainly a breathtaking tour, lasting a whole day with magnificent landscapes. The recommended period for visiting would be during the dry season, which runs from May to October. However, it is possible to visit the place all year round, but swimming during the rainy season may be restricted due to the strong current in the area. Don't leave the Couros Falls out of your itinerary as it is a must-see. For this tour it is mandatory to contact a local guide in accordance with theSEMAD. The place is huge and the guide will point out all the attractions that the Couros Falls have to offer with all the necessary safety.

Tips

  • Have lunch at the Dona Eleusa restaurant. Homemade food worth R$40 per buffet released
  • Mandatory guide
  • Avoid periods of heavy rain
  • Go with sunscreen and a sun hat, there are not many shadows in the place
  • Drive carefully on the dirt road as there is not much maintenance. Pay extra attention in case of rain to avoid jams
  • Do not swim near the Parafuso waterfall as there is a strong current that pulls you downwards.
