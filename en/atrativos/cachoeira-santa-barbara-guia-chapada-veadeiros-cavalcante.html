Santa Barbara Waterfall

Distance from Alto Paraíso: 130 km (29km of dirt road)

Trail Distance (round trip): 3 km

Difficulty Level: Easy

Attractions:

- Santa Barbarinha Waterfall

- Santa Bárbara Waterfall

Visiting period:

Whole year (avoid on very rainy days)

Mandatory guide

The Santa Bárbara Waterfall, which has turquoise blue water, is the main destination for visitors to Chapada dos Veadeiros. It is located in the city of Cavalcante in the Kalunga community, a quilombola land of more than 250 untouched and preserved hectares. From Alto Paraíso it is approximately 130 km away, 30 km of which is dirt road.

Kalunga Community

It is one of the largest quilombola remnants in Brazil, located in the Chapada dos Veadeiros region, forming and constituting its culture for more than 300 years. Today, this community primarily lives on tourism. The collection of this income is spent on environmental preservation and above all family support and maintenance of local culture. Kalunga means sacred place of protection. Therefore, much respect for this village of great strength.

Easy walk but long car journey

Upon arriving at the community, cars must remain parked at the CAT (tourist service center). Visitors must therefore walk to the beginning of the community transfer, which takes transport on a road that only passes through 4x4 cars, a journey that lasts approximately 5km.  Upon arriving at the beginning of the trail, it is a light walk that will initially pass by a river for about 1.5km. Above all, it is worth remembering that most of the time the trail is done under the sun, so it is important to take a hat and protection. It takes around 20 minutes to reach the first attraction, Santa Barbarinha, a mini version of Santa Bárbara with transparent waters and an incredible pool. Unfortunately, for conservation reasons, bathing is prohibited. So you should just take photos. Moving forward, straight ahead, will be the largest monument in Chapada dos Veadeiros, the Santa Bárbara waterfall. With 30 meters of free fall, the waterfall has an incredible pool with different shades of turquoise and crystal clear waters, reminiscent of a swimming pool. It delights our eyes and shows us its magic, truly a paradise in the middle of the savannah. For those who like diving and taking photos, this place is the right place. The attraction draws more attention due to the color of its pool than the waterfall itself.

Maximum time allowed and people access limit

It is only allowed to stay for one hour at Cachoeira Santa Barbara, unfortunately, due to the large number of tourists who visit the place. Furthermore, only 300 people can visit the Santa Bárbara waterfall daily, so if the number of visitors exceeds what is allowed, the trip will be wasted. This measure was imposed by IBAMA as a way of preserving the site. Only 50 people can be at the waterfall, so queues for the transfer are formed and groups will only be released as soon as others return.  The best time to visit is between 11 am and 2 pm, which is when the sun hits the well and the water really shows its intense blue color.

Tickets, visiting period and mandatory guide

During rainy periods, visiting is not recommended as the attraction may be closed at any time as a safety measure to avoid waterspout tragedies. We strongly recommend that you avoid this tour during holidays and high season due to the large number of visitors as there is a high chance of arriving and not having any space left on the day. Today it is possible to avoid this type of inconvenience by purchasing your ticket in advance via the website.Kalunga.

Other attractions to visit on the same day

As the time allowed for Santa Bárbara waterfall is only one hour, it is possible to visit two other attractions that are also in the Kalunga community, which are: Capivara waterfall or Candaru waterfall. To do this, you need to arrive early to enjoy them all.

Tips

  • Have lunch in the Kalunga community, without a doubt the best place to have lunch in the region. All food is organic, with meat or vegan options. Cerrado fruits and typical foods. The stove is wood burning giving that special taste. A great gastronomic experience, richness of the Chapada dos Veadeiros region. The price today is R$30 with buffet available.
  • Visit theHélia's baron the way there or on the way back. The place is a Macaw sanctuary and visitors can get up close to feed the animals and take photos. There is a small stipend fee of R$2 per person.
